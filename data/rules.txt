PRICE VALIDATION RULES - Desk note (updated daily)
Last updated: 2026-06-18 - Author: Senior Middle Office

These rules are applied by the agent at review time.
They may be edited each morning without changing any code.

1. DAILY VARIATION (Equities / FX)
   - An absolute change of 5% or less is considered normal -> APPROVED.
   - Above 5%, the move must be justified by a market event
     (see the instrument's market context).
     - If a credible event explains the move -> APPROVED.
     - Otherwise -> ESCALATE.

2. BONDS
   - Any change above 3% on a bond is abnormal by default.
   - If no credit or rates event justifies it -> REJECTED.

3. STALE PRICE
   - If the price has not been refreshed for more than 1 business day -> ESCALATE.

4. DIVERGENCE VS REFERENCE SOURCE
   - If the gap vs the reference price (vendor / consensus) exceeds 2% -> ESCALATE.

5. SENSITIVE CASES (PRIORITY RULE)
   - For any ambiguous case, or when multiple rules conflict,
     NEVER decide alone: always escalate to a human validator.
   - The agent instructs the decision; a human validates it.
